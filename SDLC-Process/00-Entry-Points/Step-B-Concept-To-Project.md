# Step B: Concept to Project

## Overview

| Attribute | Value |
| --- | --- |
| **Step Number** | B |
| **Phase** | Project & Opportunity Preliminary |
| **Previous Step** | Step A: Intake Process |

## Purpose

This step formalises the transition from an approved concept to an official project. The concept is refined, documented, and prepared for formal project initiation with appropriate governance structures.

## Activities

### B.1 Project Charter Finalisation

**Owner:** Program Management  
**Duration:** 3-5 business days

#### Process Steps:

1. **Project Charter Development**
   - Complete all required sections:
     - **Project Title and Code:** Unique identifier and descriptive name
     - **Project Sponsor:** Executive sponsor with authority
     - **Project Manager:** Assigned PM/Program Manager
     - **Business Justification:** Problem statement and opportunity
     - **Project Objectives:** SMART objectives (Specific, Measurable, Achievable, Relevant, Time-bound)
     - **High-Level Requirements:** Key functional and non-functional requirements
     - **Success Criteria:** Measurable definitions of success
     - **Preliminary Scope:** What is included and explicitly excluded
     - **Major Deliverables:** Key tangible outputs
     - **Key Milestones:** Critical decision points and dates
     - **Budget Estimate:** Order-of-magnitude cost range
     - **Risk Summary:** Top 5 risks and mitigation strategies
     - **Assumptions:** Key assumptions about resources, technology, and constraints
     - **Dependencies:** External dependencies on other projects or teams
   - Ensure executive sponsorship commitment
   - Validate resource availability commitments
2. **Project Charter Review and Approval**
   - Present to project governance board
   - Address feedback and questions
   - Obtain formal approval signatures
   - Distribute approved charter to stakeholders

#### Templates and Tools:

- Project Charter Template
- RACI Matrix Template
- Project Management Information System (PMIS)

#### Quality Checks:

- ✓ All charter sections complete and reviewed
- ✓ SMART objectives defined
- ✓ Budget estimates validated by finance
- ✓ Resource commitments confirmed
- ✓ Formal approval obtained

### B.2 Stakeholder Analysis and Engagement Planning

**Owner:** Program Management  
**Duration:** 2-3 business days

#### Process Steps:

1. **Stakeholder Identification**
   - Identify all individuals and groups affected by the project
   - Categorise stakeholders:
     - **Primary:** Direct involvement and decision-making authority
     - **Secondary:** Interested but not directly involved
     - **Key:** Can significantly impact or be impacted by the project
   - Document stakeholder roles, interests, and influence levels
2. **Power-Interest Grid Analysis**
   - Plot stakeholders on a 2x2 grid:
     - **High Power, High Interest:** Manage Closely
     - **High Power, Low Interest:** Keep Satisfied
     - **Low Power, High Interest:** Keep Informed
     - **Low Power, Low Interest:** Monitor
   - Identify potential champions and resistors
   - Plan engagement strategies for each stakeholder segment
3. **Communication Plan Development**
   - Define communication frequency and channels
   - Tailor messaging for different stakeholder groups
   - Establish feedback mechanisms
   - Create communication schedule and responsibilities

#### Templates and Tools:

- Stakeholder Register Template
- Power-Interest Grid Template
- Communications Plan Template

#### Quality Checks:

- ✓ All key stakeholders identified
- ✓ Power-interest analysis complete
- ✓ Engagement strategies defined
- ✓ Communication plan approved

---

### B.3 Preliminary Risk Assessment

**Owner:** Program Management  
**Duration:** 2-3 business days

#### Process Steps:

1. **Risk Identification Workshop**
   - Conduct brainstorming sessions with key stakeholders
   - Use risk identification techniques:
     - SWOT Analysis (Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats)
     - PESTLE Analysis (Political, Economic, Social, Technological, Legal, Environmental)
     - Lessons Learned from similar projects
     - Expert judgment
   - Document identified risks in the risk register
2. **Risk Analysis and Prioritisation**
   - Assess each risk using a risk matrix:
     - **Probability:** Likelihood of occurrence (Low, Medium, High)
     - **Impact:** Severity of consequences (Low, Medium, High)
     - **Risk Score:** Probability × Impact
   - Prioritise risks based on risk scores
   - Categorise risks (Technical, Organizational, External, Project Management)
3. **Risk Response Planning**
   - For high and medium risks, define response strategies:
     - **Avoid:** Change project plan to eliminate risk
     - **Mitigate:** Reduce probability or impact
     - **Transfer:** Shift risk to a third party
     - **Accept:** Acknowledge and monitor
   - Assign risk owners and action plans
   - Establish risk monitoring triggers and indicators

#### Templates and Tools:

- Risk Register Template
- Risk Matrix Template
- SWOT Analysis Template

#### Quality Checks:

- ✓ Comprehensive risk identification completed
- ✓ All risks assessed and prioritised
- ✓ Response strategies defined for high/medium risks
- ✓ Risk owners assigned

### B.4 Governance Structure Setup

**Owner:** Program Management  
**Duration:** 2-3 business days

#### Process Steps:

1. **Project Governance Framework Design**
   - Define governance levels:
     - **Steering Committee:** Strategic oversight and major decision-making
     - **Project Board:** Tactical guidance and approvals
     - **Working Groups:** Specialized focus areas (Technical, Business, Compliance)
   - Establish decision-making authority levels
   - Define escalation paths and procedures
2. **Meeting Structure Planning**
   - Schedule regular governance meetings:
     - **Steering Committee:** Monthly or quarterly
     - **Project Board:** Bi-weekly or monthly
     - **Working Groups:** Weekly or bi-weekly
   - Define meeting agendas and expected outputs
   - Establish reporting templates and formats
3. **Governance Documentation**
   - Create governance charter documenting:
     - Roles and responsibilities
     - Decision-making processes
     - Escalation procedures
     - Meeting schedules and formats
     - Reporting requirements
   - Obtain approval from governance board
   - Distribute to all stakeholders

#### Templates and Tools:

- Governance Charter Template
- Meeting Agenda Templates
- Status Report Templates

#### Quality Checks:

- ✓ Governance structure defined and documented
- ✓ Roles and responsibilities clear
- ✓ Meeting schedules established
- ✓ Escalation procedures defined

---

### B.5 High-Level Planning

**Owner:** Program Management  
**Duration:** 3-5 business days

#### Process Steps:

1. **Project Roadmap Development**
   - Create high-level timeline with major phases:
     - Phase 0: Project Initiation
     - Phase 1: Discovery and Planning
     - Phase 2: Architecture and Design
     - Phase 3: Development
     - Phase 4: Testing and Validation
     - Phase 5: Deployment and Transition
   - Identify key milestones and deliverables
   - Map dependencies between phases
2. **Resource Planning**
   - Estimate resource needs by role and phase:
     - Development team (Developers, QA, DevOps)
     - Architecture and design (Solution Architects, UX Designers)
     - Project management and support
   - Identify skill gaps and training needs
   - Plan for resource ramp-up and ramp-down
3. **Budget Estimation**
   - Develop order-of-magnitude budget estimates:
     - Labor costs (internal and external)
     - Technology and infrastructure costs
     - Training and development costs
     - Contingency reserves (10-20% of total)
   - Validate estimates with finance and procurement
   - Obtain budget approval from sponsors
4. **Procurement Planning**
   - Identify goods and services to be procured
   - Determine procurement methods and timelines
   - Engage procurement team for vendor selection support

#### Templates and Tools:

- Project Roadmap Template
- Resource Planning Template
- Budget Template
- Procurement Plan Template

#### Quality Checks:

- ✓ High-level roadmap complete with milestones
- ✓ Resource estimates validated
- ✓ Budget approved by sponsors
- ✓ Procurement needs identified

## Key Decisions

1. **Project Charter Approval**
   - **Decision Point:** End of Activity B.1
   - **Decision Maker:** Project Governance Board
   - **Criteria:** Business case validated, objectives clear, resources committed
2. **Governance Structure Approval**
   - **Decision Point:** End of Activity B.4
   - **Decision Maker:** Steering Committee
   - **Criteria:** Appropriate oversight, clear decision-making authority
3. **Proceed to Project Initiation**
   - **Decision Point:** End of Step B
   - **Decision Maker:** Project Sponsor and Governance Board
   - **Criteria:** Charter approved, governance established, preliminary planning complete

## Risks and Mitigations

| Risk | Impact | Mitigation Strategy |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Stakeholder misalignment on objectives | High | Conduct stakeholder workshops early; validate objectives with all key stakeholders |
| Inadequate resource commitments | High | Obtain firm commitments from resource managers; escalate to sponsors if needed |
| Incomplete risk identification | Medium | Use multiple risk identification techniques; involve diverse perspectives |
| Budget estimates significantly off | Medium | Use analogous estimating from similar projects; add appropriate contingency |
| Governance structure too heavy or too light | Medium | Tailor governance to project size and complexity; review and adjust as needed |
