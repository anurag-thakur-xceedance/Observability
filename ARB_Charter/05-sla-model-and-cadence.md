## 1. Standard Cadence

The ARB meets on a **fortnightly (every two weeks)** basis. The standard cadence is:

| Activity | Timing |
|---|---|
| Intake submission deadline | 5 business days before the scheduled session |
| EA CoE circulates decision papers | 3 business days before the session |
| ARB session | Fortnightly, standing calendar invite |
| Decision records published | Within 2 business days of the session |
| ADR finalised and published | Within 5 business days of the session |

---

## 2. Standard Track SLAs

From the point of intake confirmation:

| Milestone | SLA |
|---|---|
| Triage and track assignment | 2 business days |
| Decision paper circulated to members | 3 business days before next session |
| Decision issued | At the next scheduled fortnightly session |
| Maximum elapsed time (standard) | 15 business days |

If the submission arrives too late to make the next session, it is scheduled for the following session. The EA CoE will advise the submitting team of the expected session date at triage.

---

## 3. Urgent Track SLA

The 48-hour urgent track is available for decisions that cannot wait for the standard cadence (see [Chapter 4, Section 4 — Fast-Track Intake for Urgent Matters](04-intake-criteria-and-materiality-thresholds.md#4-fast-track-intake-for-urgent-matters)).

| Milestone | SLA |
|---|---|
| Intake form received with urgency flag | T+0 |
| EA CoE acknowledges and convenes quorum | T+4 hours (business hours) |
| ARB async or synchronous review completes | T+48 hours |
| Decision record issued | T+48 hours |
| Formal ratification at next standard session | Next fortnightly session |

The 48-hour clock runs from receipt of a complete intake form. Incomplete submissions reset the clock from the point of completion.

---

## 4. Non-Material Exception Recording

Decisions recorded by exception (see [Chapter 4, Section 3 — Non-Material Decisions](04-intake-criteria-and-materiality-thresholds.md#3-non-material-decisions--recording-by-exception)) are acknowledged by the EA CoE within **3 business days**. No formal ARB session is required.

---

## 5. SLA Reporting

The EA CoE publishes a quarterly SLA compliance report to the ARB Chair and senior leadership, covering:

- Volume of decisions by track (standard, urgent, exception).
- SLA compliance rate per track.
- Average elapsed time from intake to decision.
- Number of decisions escalated or disputed.
