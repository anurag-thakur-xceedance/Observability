## 1. Chair

The ARB is chaired by the **Senior EVP / President** or their designated delegate.

The Chair:

- Has final decision-making authority on any matter brought to the ARB.
- May delegate authority to the EA CoE Head for defined categories of non-contentious or routine ARB decisions, subject to ratification at the next standard session.
- Casts the deciding vote in the event of a tied vote among standing members.
- Is responsible for ensuring the ARB operates within its charter and that decisions are made in a timely manner consistent with the SLA commitments in [Chapter 5 — SLA Model and Cadence](05-sla-model-and-cadence.md).

In the Chair's absence, they must designate an acting Chair in advance. An undesignated absence does not defer the session; the senior-most standing member present assumes acting Chair authority for that session.

---

## 2. Standing Members

The following roles hold permanent standing membership on the ARB:

| Role | Domain |
|---|---|
| Head of Product Engineering (or delegate) | Product Engineering |
| EDGE Architecture Lead (or delegate) | EDGE Platform |
| Corporate IT Architecture Lead (or delegate) | Corp IT |
| EA CoE Head | Enterprise Architecture (secretariat, non-voting) |

Standing members are expected to:

- Attend or send a prepared delegate to every session.
- Review circulated decision papers in advance of the session.
- Declare conflicts of interest on decisions where their domain has a direct stake.
- Contribute domain-level risk and impact assessment during deliberation.

The EA CoE Head acts as secretariat: managing the agenda, distributing papers, recording minutes, and publishing decisions. The EA CoE Head does not vote.

---

## 3. Invited Members

The EA CoE may invite subject-matter experts, domain leads, or vendor representatives to specific sessions where their input is relevant to the decision under review. Invited members:

- Participate in discussion on the specific item(s) for which they are invited.
- Do not hold voting rights.
- May be required to leave the session once their item is concluded, at the Chair's discretion.

---

## 4. Quorum

| Track | Minimum Quorum |
|---|---|
| Standard fortnightly session | Chair (or acting Chair) plus two standing members |
| 48-hour urgent track | Chair (or acting Chair) plus two standing members (async acceptable) |

A session that does not meet quorum may not issue binding decisions. Agenda items from a quorum-failed session are carried forward to the next session with priority scheduling.

---

## 5. Delegated Authority

The Chair may formally delegate authority to the EA CoE for categories of decision that are:

- Within an already-established standard (e.g., approving a new service that uses only approved technology components and follows documented integration patterns).
- Below a defined complexity threshold agreed between the Chair and the EA CoE Head.
- Subject to retrospective ratification at the next standard ARB session.

Delegated decisions are recorded in the ARB minutes and carry the same binding status as a full ARB decision. The EA CoE does not hold delegated authority for decisions that involve new technology adoption, significant resilience or SLA impact, or any matter where a standing member has raised an objection.

---

## 6. Membership Review

Membership composition is reviewed annually by the Chair. Changes to standing membership require Chair approval and are recorded in an updated version of this charter.
